About This Document

Here's a quick rundown of what the Testing Team does and how we fit into everything else going on around here. It tells you what we do, who we work closely with, and how we help keep things running smoothly.

And if you're curious about anything else, feel free to pop over and ask.

Our Team Values in Action

‚ñº

Ignite Growth

  • We continuously learn from bugs found and use those insights to improve our testing strategies
  • We share what works (and what doesn't) in testing approaches so everyone benefits from our learnings
  • We challenge ourselves to find better ways to ensure quality, not just follow test scripts

Empower Through Innovation

  • We take time to understand what features really need to do before testing them
  • We look for practical testing approaches that balance thoroughness with delivery speed
  • When a testing approach isn't catching issues, we're willing to adapt and try different methods

Always Go Beyond

  • We put ourselves in users' shoes when testing features, thinking about real-world usage scenarios
  • We respond quickly to testing requests and bug reports, knowing that delays impact releases
  • We focus our energy on testing that genuinely improves quality and prevents issues in production

Trust Starts With Us

  • When we see potential quality issues (critical bugs, regression risks, usability problems), we flag them early rather than waiting
  • We keep teams informed about testing progress and blockers, even when it means delaying releases
  • When we commit to testing timelines, we do everything we can to follow through
  • We own our missed bugs and testing gaps and focus on improving our processes rather than making excuses

Win Together

  • We value input from Product, Engineering, and users - Testing brings quality expertise, others bring technical and business knowledge
  • We work closely with all teams to ensure quality releases that meet user expectations
  • We celebrate the whole team's success when we ship high-quality features
  • We approach every quality discussion with respect and professionalism, even when raising concerns about release readiness
